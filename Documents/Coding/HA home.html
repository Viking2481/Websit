Welcome Fellow Spartans!


What is your favorite helmet from the Halo Universe? Does it describe your personality or the look of the helmet just suits you? This is a website that I, Viking 2481, created to help people make helmet art for them to show of to friends and family! I essentially use concept art or other community members art and sketch over them, allowing for nice custom and unique art piece for every individual out there!




How It's Done


When I start my helmets off, I first need the art that I'll be basing mine off of. When I have have that, I center it, margin it off, and then change the size of the document to 800 by 800 pixels and set 200 pixels per inch. Once I do that, I erase the background of the helmet and add three layers below the helmet: black, grey, and white backgrounds. Once I fill those layers with their colors, I then add 5-7 lays on top of the helmet. These layers are (in order) lines, primary color, secondary color, miscellaneous colors, visor lines, and then finally the visor itself





Phase 1: Shaping it


Phase 1 is the hardest part of each project. This is when I go over every line of detail to shape of the helmet. I first start off by going over the outside and then work from top to bottom. When I'm done, I then do the details of the visor. After all of the lines have been traced, I then duplicate both the lines and visor lines layers and merge them together with their copies to make the lines stand out more. Once this is done, I then move onto Phase 2.





Phase 2: Adding the Primary Color


As the Phases go on, the get easier and easier. This Phase is simply adding the primary color to the helmet. Sometime on art there is not secondary colr or just not enough, so sometimes the area of the primary color doesn't match the original arts.





Phase 3: Adding the Secondary Color


This is were we add the secondary color to the helmet. By now we are soon to finishing and the helmet is starting start to look nice and snazy.





Phase 4: Adding Extra Colors and Graphics


Now we add the color to parts of the helmet that are not the primary, secondary, or visor color. This is typically filled in with shades of gray. If there are UNSC or any other types of logos, this is the time that I insert them





Phase 5: The Visor and the Final Product


Now to wrap things up, we add the visor color. Some helmets might have some strips of a very transparent black to make the visor and helmet seem just a tad bit 3D. Now, the helmet is finished and yours to share to the world!
